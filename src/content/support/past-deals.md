## What are past deals?
Agents can log properties they have previously sold on WHOMA. Each deal can include the property address, completion date, the agent's role, and optionally the sale price.

## Why add past deals?
Past deals help agents build a more evidence-based professional profile. They give homeowners more context about the agent's experience and local activity.

## How does seller verification work?
After logging a deal, the agent can enter the seller's email. WHOMA sends the seller an email asking them to confirm the agent sold their property.

If the seller confirms, the deal can show as verified on the agent's profile or evidence record.

## What happens if the seller disputes it?
The deal is marked as disputed and does not appear as verified. The agent is notified by email.

## Does WHOMA publish seller contact details?
Seller contact details should be used only for verification. They should not be shown publicly as part of an agent profile.

## Are sale prices required?
Sale prices are optional where the product allows them. Agents should only provide accurate information and should not invent property data or prices.
